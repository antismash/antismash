⚠️ Important Notice

This analysis is designed for individual bacterial or fungal genomes only. Results may not be meaningful for:

  • Metagenomes or metagenomic assemblies
  • Mobile genetic elements (plasmids, phages/viruses)
  • Incomplete genomes

For accurate results, ensure your input represents a complete or near-complete single bacterial or fungal genome.

Multiple Testing Consideration: If you are assessing multiple BGC regions in your genome, consider applying multiple testing correction (e.g., Bonferroni or FDR) to the reported P-values to control for false discovery rates.

Understanding the Results

Statistical Significance

Low P-values (< 0.05) suggest the BGC has significantly different codon usage from its genomic context, potentially indicating horizontal gene transfer or atypical expression/translation frequency. High P-values (≥ 0.05) suggest the BGC has similar codon usage to its genomic context.

Cosine Distance

Measures the angular distance between codon frequency vectors. Values closer to 0 indicate more similar codon usage patterns, while values closer to 1 indicate more different patterns.

Spearman Correlation

Measures the rank correlation between codon frequencies. Values closer to 1 indicate similar ranking of codon preferences, while values closer to -1 indicate opposite rankings.
